Discussion: Software Supply Chain Hacks

In 2023, within a brief period, 400 malicious packages were incorporated into PyPI, the official packager manager for the popular Python software development language. Once a developer installed the package via PyPI, it created a malicious JavaScript extension that would load every time one of the popular browsers was opened on the device. This created a persistent foothold on the victim machine. This malware would monitor the machine’s clipboard for cryptocurrency addresses and if found, the address would be replaced with the attacker’s deposit information. This attack impacted hundreds of developer machines. In 2016, a similar attack was successfully executed more than 40,000 times before it was discovered.

Discussion: SLA and Supplier Failure

Sometimes supplier organizations experience profound consequences when violations occur. In 2021, for example, Robinhood, an immensely popular mobile brokerage technology firm, received the largest Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA) penalty to date—a blistering US$70 million. This fine was related to an inability to provide a stable stocks and options trading platform to clients in 2020. Many days with complete outages were noted. Sadly, one young trader who racked up losses during the outages even ended his own life, according to FINRA. When businesses fail to adequately safeguard confidentiality, integrity, and the availability of systems and software, irreversible damage can occur.

Note: Since this unfortunate event, Robinhood has taken several steps to bolster confidentiality, integrity, and availability of offerings through large security and technology investments. Robinhood has also taken extra steps to ensure customers have the support they need when application issues arise by more than tripling customer support staff.1
