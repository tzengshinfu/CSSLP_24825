Discussion: Verifying Secure Mobile Designs

The OWASP Mobile Application Security (MAS) project provides a security standard for mobile apps (OWASP MASVS) and a companion test guide (OWASP MASTG). Links are provided below.

Instructions

Answer the following question. Be prepared to discuss your thoughts with the class.

OWASP MASVS: https://mas.owasp.org/MASVS/

OWASP MASTG: https://mas.owasp.org/MASTG/

  • What are the distinct purposes of each guide?
  • Are there similarities with the OWASP MAS documents and NIST SP 800-53B and NIST SP 800-53A? Explain.
  • Are good design requirements testable? What does that mean to you? 

Discussion: Multifactor Authentication (MFA)

Almost everyone has encountered a situation where they have entered their authentication credentials and received a text with a numerical login code. Alternatively, they may have used a dedicated authentication app to receive codes. Think about MFA use cases and implementations. 

Instructions

Consider the following questions. Be prepared to share your thoughts with the class.

  • Do you use MFA for remote login?
  • Do you require it on your administrative consoles?
  • Do you use it to access code repositories?
  • Do you use it for accessing emails?
  • Can multifactor authentication help protect against spoofing (e.g., impersonating, masquerading, piggybacking, mimicking)? 

Discussion: Microkernel

Sometimes it would be beneficial to provide a microkernel—a kernel that contains only the minimum level of required functions, features, and mechanisms of a fully-fledged operating system.
