Domain 4: Secure Software Architecture
and Design

In this domain, the many considerations for architecting and designing secure software will be discussed, including threat modeling, which is a critical security-oriented activity undertaken to identify and understand threats and mitigations. Attack surface analysis, with a focus on identification of the entry points into the software, will follow. Threat intelligence involves gathering information about existing or upcoming threats obtained through internal resources, subscription to security alerts from advisories and bulletins, and information sharing with communities.

Architecture and cloud computing will also be discussed with service and deployment models considerations, shared security responsibilities, mobile architecture, and common weaknesses. Other design topics include interface security for application programming interfaces (APIs), web services, and federated access models.

Design and architecture are early-stage activities within the system and software life cycle. If security practitioners have the fortune to engage stakeholders at the beginning, they should embrace and appreciate the opportunity to deliver business value while optimizing solutions for confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

Learning Objectives

In this domain, you will:

  • Describe architecture and security-relevant design patterns.
  • Recognize the security criteria of interfaces design.
  • Compare and distinguish various authentication and authorization mechanisms.
  • Describe credential management.
  • Identify the principles and tools used in network security.
  • Describe the methods used to maintain database security.
  • Identify the threat modeling process, tools, and methodologies.
  • Indicate the process of attack surface evaluation and management.
  • Discuss threat intelligence and sources for cyberthreat information.
  • Describe the architecture risk assessment process.
  • Recognize nonfunctional security properties and constraints.
  • Identify secure operational architecture considerations.
