Domain 7: Secure Software Deployment, Operations, and Maintenance

The absence of controls, or inadequate implementation of safeguards during software integration, installation, and deployment stages, can adversely impact the operational security of software. Change controls (e.g., secure configurations) and access management concepts such as least privilege are critical to maintaining operational security.

Updates to organizational infrastructure require planning to maintain continuity of operations. When changes are not planned, controlled, and monitored, organizations fail to properly manage risk. The seemingly trivial task of updating software infrastructure or replacing a network cable could potentially cripple critical infrastructure. Security verification testing conducted post-deployment can help identify missed vulnerabilities and enhance confidence that production systems are free from defects.

Information security continuous monitoring (ISCM) provides organizations situational awareness regarding production infrastructure. ISCM, when combined with continuity planning, enables organizations to respond to security incidents in a timely and appropriate manner. Organizations should establish patch and vulnerability management policies and procedures that leverage enterprise change control. Timing, prioritizing, and testing infrastructure changes are important operations activities. 

Learning Objectives

In this domain you will:

  • Describe operational risk analysis in the context of ISO 31000 series.
  • Describe Security Configuration Management (SecCM).
  • Identify the elements of the Secure Continuous Integration and Continuous Delivery (CI/CD) pipeline.
  • Explain the application security toolchain.
  • Identify the steps to identify app vulnerabilities.
  • Compare and distinguish the common methods to store and manage security data.
  • Describe secure installation processes and methods.
  • Explain secure software activation mechanisms.
  • Identify the steps and methods in the Authorization to Operate (ATO) process.
  • Explain how to perform information security continuous monitoring.
  • Describe the phases involved in an incident response plan.
  • Relate the patch management process to overall software security practices.
  • Describe the methods and tools used for vulnerability management.
  • Describe how controls contribute to protecting applications during runtime.
  • Compare and distinguish how business continuity and disaster recovery plans support continuity of operations.
  • Explain and differentiate service-level agreements (SLA), service-level objectives (SLO), and service-level indicators (SLI) to achieve supplier continuity.
