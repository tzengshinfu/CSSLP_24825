Domain 1 Review

Different software security approaches may result in different outcomes. Regardless of framework, standard, or methodology selection, security practitioners must ensure that the implementation of secure system and software design results in a consistent, repeatable process that can be measured and monitored across the life cycle. At the heart of every security endeavor are considerations for confidentiality, integrity, and availabilityâ€”software security is no exception. Various secure design concepts and design principles are considered at the beginning of system engineering efforts to ensure resulting applications are cyber resilient with adequate protections against unauthorized disclosure, alteration, or destruction of data.

Applications must be architected with business and security goals from their genesis. GRC helps deal with integrating business, risk, and compliance considerations. Production applications have added complexities such as jurisdiction and resource allocations within systems that are best considered as early as possible within the development life cycle.

Software security design principles should be leveraged in software development projects to design and implement secure applications. These principles are collections of desirable application properties, behaviors, designs, and implementation practices that attempt to reduce the likelihood of threat realization and impact should that threat be realized. The better the secure design implementation accomplishes adequate security the less dependent the production systems and software will be on the addition of security control safeguards.
