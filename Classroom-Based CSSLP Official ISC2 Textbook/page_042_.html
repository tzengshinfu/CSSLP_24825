Multiple Choice Question Group

Question 1

1. Which security control type best mitigates damage after an incident? (D4.1, L4.1) 

A. Limitation controls
B. Preventive controls
C. Detective controls
marked correct D. Corrective controls
Feedback

Your answer is correct.

The correct answer is D. Corrective controls restore the system or process back to the state prior to a harmful event. As such, they contain the damage and prevent further spread of the incident.

Incorrect answer feedback: Limitation controls are not specifically focused on mitigating damage after an incident. Preventive controls are aimed at preventing incidents from occurring in the first place. Detective controls are focused on detecting and identifying incidents or security breaches as they occur or shortly after they happen.

marked correct A. Discretionary access control (DAC)
B. Nondiscretionary access control (NDAC)
C. Role-based access control (RBAC).
D. Attribute-based access control (ABAC).
Feedback

Your answer is correct.

The correct answer is A. The question describes nondiscretionary access control (NDAC), often referred to as Mandatory access control (MAC); discretionary access control (DAC) is the opposite of NDAC.

Incorrect answer feedback: NDAC is an authorization mechanism through which permissions are granted based on predetermined rules and policies.
